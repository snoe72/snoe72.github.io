About Me

Education

  • Ph.D. Anthropology, University of California, Santa Barbara (expected 2025)
  • M.A. Applied Anthropology, University of Maryland
  • B.A. Anthropology, Mercyhurst University

Research Interests

I study human-environment interactions with a focus on subsistence strategies, animal domestication, and risk mitigation in the face of environmental and social change. My research explores how ancient societies adapted to shifting ecologies, developed food systems, and maintained cooperation and resilience in times of stress.

Methods Expertise

I apply stable isotope analysis, ancient DNA (aDNA), zooarchaeology, and radiocarbon dating to reconstruct past diets, mobility patterns, and human-animal relationships. My work integrates archaeological and scientific datasets to investigate domestication, hunting intensification, and pastoral transitions. I also use statistical and computational tools, including R, Python, and MATLAB, for quantitative analysis, data visualization, and ecological modeling.

Regional Expertise

My research centers on the Central Andes, with additional work in the North American Midwest, particularly Mississippian mound sites.

  • Research Overview

    The Domestication of Camelids in the Lake Titicaca Basin, Peru : Conducted at the CARI facility in Puno, Peru, this research examines the transition from specialized hunting practices to the early management of camelids during the Archaic Period (9.0–3.5 ka).

    Subsistence Strategies in Mississippian Societies : This research, focusing on the American Bottom and Central Illinois River Valley between the 11th and 14th centuries, investigates the effects of the Medieval Climatic Anomaly on agricultural development and population dynamics, and the resultant influence on hunting strategies.

    Foodways in Historic California : This project examines the impact of the Spanish colonial mission system on Indigenous dietary customs in Alta California, uncovering the persistence of Native culinary traditions in the face of colonial influences.
